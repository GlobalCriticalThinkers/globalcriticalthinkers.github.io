Why GCT exists

A competition built on one belief.

We created GCT because we believe competitions should reward thinking, not memorization. Most competitions reward the loudest voice in the room. We wanted one that rewards the clearest one.

GCT started as a conversation between a small group of people who kept noticing the same gap: students who could argue well, but hadn't been asked to actually reason well. So we built the competition we wished we'd had. One real-world challenge, drawn from business, technology, policy, or another field entirely. Four roles that force real division of labor. A defense round with nowhere to hide. There is rarely one perfect answer, only the strength of the reasoning behind it. Nothing scripted, nothing rehearsed.

Where the thinking

Analyst

Breaks the challenge down to its real problem, not the one sitting on the surface.

02

Evidence Analyst

Tests every assumption against the data. If it doesn't hold up, it doesn't go forward.

03

Strategist

04

Presenter

Everything the team built comes down to this. Clear thinking, spoken plainly.

The presentation

The idea meets the room.

  • 10 minutes to present the full recommendation, unaided by a script.
  • Any team member can be asked to answer, not only the Presenter.
  • Clarity over polish. Judges are trained to reward substance, not delivery flourish.

The defense

Every idea gets challenged.
Every ego gets left at the door.

The judges push back, hard. The team holds its ground or changes its mind in real time. Both are wins, if the reasoning behind them is sound.

15-minute open cross-examination
Judged on reasoning, not just conclusions
