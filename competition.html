Competition Overview

One challenge. One day. One standard of judgment.

Every participant works through the same real-world challenge, under the same conditions, using the same evaluation framework — first on their own, then as part of a team — regardless of background, school, or prior experience.

Assessment

How You're Evaluated

Great decisions begin with great reasoning.

Every participant is assessed at each stage of the competition — individual and collaborative — using one standardized evaluation framework designed to measure not only outcomes, but also the quality of thinking behind every decision.

That framework is built around a deliberate choice: confidence, memorization, and polish are not what earns marks here. What is examined is how a participant actually reasons — how a problem is framed, how evidence is weighed, and how an answer holds up once it's questioned.

The framework

Four disciplines
judges are looking for.

Behind the scoring sheet is a simple structure. At every stage of the competition, judges are watching for the same four disciplines — each one a different way of testing whether your thinking actually holds up.

01

Analysis

The discipline of seeing past the obvious framing. You learn to ask what's actually being asked, before reaching for an answer.

02

Evidence

The discipline of intellectual honesty. You learn to distrust your first instinct until the data has earned your confidence.

03

Judgment

The discipline of deciding under uncertainty. You learn to commit to a position you can actually defend, not just one that sounds good.

04

Communication

The discipline of clarity. You learn that an idea only counts once someone else can follow exactly how you got there.

Round 1 — Individual Position Statement

Before collaboration comes
independent judgment.

Every participant begins alone. Before any team discussion takes place, each participant develops an independent position and presents it directly to the judging panel — a test of reasoning with no one else's opinion in the room yet.

2-minute individual presentation
Independent reasoning before collaboration
No Q&A during presentations
Individual assessment by the judging panel
2 Minutes to present an independent position before the panel

Round 2 — Round Table Discussion

Independent ideas become
collective judgment.

With individual positions on the table, the team comes together. Over 45 minutes, participants weigh each other's reasoning, challenge assumptions, and work toward one solution the whole team can stand behind — the goal is a stronger shared answer, not a winning argument.

  • Explain each position. Every participant walks the team through the reasoning behind what they presented individually.
  • Challenge assumptions. Teammates question each other's logic and evidence, not to win, but to pressure-test it.
  • Negotiate viewpoints. Differences are worked through openly until the strongest reasoning surfaces.
  • Build one consensus. The team converges on a single, well-reasoned position everyone can defend.
  • Select a presenter. Only once the team's final decision is set does the group choose who will carry it forward.

The examination

This is where thinking
either holds — or it doesn't.

Judges will find the weakest point in your reasoning and press on it. You can hold your position or change your mind — both are respected, as long as either one is earned through logic, not stubbornness or nerves.

15 minutes of direct, unscripted questioning
Judged on how you think, not how you sound

Partners

Built together with trusted partners.

Global Critical Thinkers collaborates with universities, schools, and industry organizations that share our standard for how thinking should be tested. These institutional and educational partners help shape a competition experience grounded in real academic and professional rigor.

Strategic partners contribute expertise, resources, and platforms that extend the competition's reach — from evaluation design to opportunities for participants beyond the competition day itself.

Every partnership is chosen deliberately. It's how GCT stays connected to real institutions and real standards, rather than operating in isolation. Partnership opportunities are currently open, and official partners will be named here as they're confirmed.
